Custom Exhibit Backdrops

ExhibitBackdrops.com

Exhibit Backdrops provides large format printed graphics for tradeshows and events. We analyzed and optimized the website for smooth browsing, ease of navigation, and faster checkout process.

Problem

How might we redesign ceb's website to help the user understand our product line and quickly find the printed fabric solution they need.

Goal

To be the go-to source that tradeshow goers of every experience level, budget and show rely on to complete their printed fabric display
Solution

  • Define the user Survey and interview existing customers, collect quantitative and qualitative data, test usability with new users, create user persona, explore stress cases.

  • Understanding competition Analyze competitors prioritize features like providing design templates easy to use by non-designers.

  • Simplify navigation Redesign menu to represent the product line better. Highlight design services and our tradeshow themed blog

  • Copy Use plain language, short sentences, and paragraphs simple words, bullet points, and straight forward direction. Providing explicit content, direct information, eliminating fluff, and confusion.

User Reaserch

We performed 20 user tests to better understand navigation to the Printed Fabrics page. We asked the participants to perform three simple tasks and provide verbal feedback while working through them.

  • Navigate to the Printed Fabrics category
  • Find the Printed Fabric Table Runner (Any Size)
  • Order a 28in x 80in table runner
Results The majority of users try looking for "Printed Fabrics" in the drop-down menu item "Shop By Fabric".

User Persona

Through our research, we found that our target audience is small business owners looking to grow there customer base on the trade show floor.

Scenerio

Franka has been making and selling handmade soaps and lotions with her daughters for a while. She decided its an excellent opportunity to attend a paddler show and try to expose her product to more people. She found the perfect tradeshow and signed up. She searched the web to find the ideal display to market her goods, set her apart from the crowd, and bring more people to her booth.

  • Franka Batenberg | 50y
  • Owns a hand made soap and lotion company.
  • Business has been booming recently
  • $70,000 per year
  • Merried with children
  • Goals
  • Have an excellent tradeshow display
  • Many people stoping by her booth
  • Be in/out of the show floor quickly
  • Motivations
  • Wants to grow her small business
  • Sell more product
  • Pain Points
  • People just passing her booth
  • Heavy lifting
  • Comple set up/ tear down displays
  • Shipping coast

HotJar

To furder understand our user's patterns, we installed HotJar and monitor it for a month. Here is what we found.

Desktop Click Patterns

  1. 28% of users click on products text first
  2. 14% click on the hero image (unaware it is not a link)
  3. 14% click on menu
  4. 75% scrow pass produts

Mobile Click Patterns

  1. 26% click on the hero image (unaware it is not a link)
  2. 9% of users click on products text first
  3. 6% click on menu
  4. 20% scrow pass produts
