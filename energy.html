energy.gov

While attending the Ux/Ui Bootcamp at Georgia Institute of technology, Our team was tasked with redesigning of a government website - energy.gov. During research and discovery, our team realized that the majority of users over 85% ware coming to the site looking for specific information. With user tests and navigation tests, we learned that the site had contrast issues that made titles challenging to read, often getting users frustrated. Our team reorganize the content, gave energy.com a modern look, highlighting the search bar making it easy to read and navigate.

Problem

Users were overwhelmed by the vast amount of disorganized information on the site.

Solution

Prioritize search feature, introduce a new navigation system, and redesign energy.gov with a modern twist.

Process

  1. Research & Discovery My team performed user interviews and tests.

  2. Redesign inspiration I was inspired by a user quote - when asked what energy means to them. They replied, "energy is the difference between light and dark." Also, our approach was to focus on mobile-first. We made sure the content flowed on mobile devices, as well as it did on full-sized browsers.

  3. Intuitive navigation Through testing and card sorting, we ware able to fully grasp the size of this site and give it a more logical organization and eliminate repetitions.

  4. Design System I chose an immaculate and minimal design style focusing on light and dark

  5. Sketches, wireframes and prototypes Sketched out flows, used Adobe XD to build wireframes and prototypes implementing the minimal design system.

Research & Discovery

Conducting interviews with users than watching them navigate the site led us to conclude the DoE's primary users go to the website for a specific reason, and their main frustration finding the information they set out to get. Due to the immensity of the site, they often rely entirely on the search function to find the information they need. This finding led us to redesign the home page with the search bar featured in a primary and central location.

Redesign Inspiration

The idea for the redesign comes to form a user interview. "Energy is the difference between light and dark., warm, and cold." That simple sentence captured the essence of energy, in our opinion, so we decided to center the design to this statement. The design is clean, black, and white.
