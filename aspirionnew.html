Aspirion Case Study Presentation

Slide 1: Project Overview

Redesigning Analyst Work Queue Management at Aspirion

  • Company: Aspirion – a healthcare revenue cycle company
  • Role: UX Lead
  • Goal: Redesign the analyst experience to simplify task management, reduce complexity, and support future AI integration

Slide 2: The Problem

Manual processes and high complexity

  • Analysts managed tasks using a complex AG Grid interface
  • Required advanced Excel skills, limiting who could be trained
  • High training costs and slower onboarding
  • System complexity prevented less-experienced users from being productive

Slide 3: Business Goals

Simplify the tool and reduce dependency on high-skill labor

  • Remove AG Grid to reduce reliance on Excel familiarity
  • Create a simplified, intuitive UI that lowers the barrier to entry
  • Design for a broader, more scalable workforce
  • Lay groundwork for automation and AI support

Slide 4: Research & Discovery

Understanding real workflows

  • Shadowed analysts across regions and specialties
  • Mapped out how users navigated and prioritized work
  • Identified key pain points:
    • Task duplication
    • Manual prioritization
    • Overwhelming interface

Slide 5: Design Objectives

  • Simplify interface and reduce cognitive load
  • Support multiple roles and work types
  • Enable automated task assignment in the future
  • Optimize for speed, clarity, and ease of use

Slide 6: Key Design Decisions

  • Removed AG Grid to eliminate Excel-style complexity
  • Introduced a card-based queue view with clear task hierarchy
  • Designed dynamic filters and role-based views
  • Integrated feedback from analysts into every iteration

Slide 7: Impact & Outcomes

  • Reduced time-to-proficiency by 22%
  • Cut manual sorting and assignment by introducing smarter defaults
  • Made the tool usable by less-specialized staff, reducing future labor costs
  • Provided a scalable foundation for AI queue management

Slide 8: Takeaways

  • Simplifying doesn’t mean dumbing down—it means removing friction
  • Designing for accessibility helps scalability
  • Deep user research + iterative design = impactful transformation
