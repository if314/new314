Context

For over two decades, Aspirion has helped healthcare providers maximize revenue recovery by focusing on challenging reimbursements. The existing training system, however, fostered mistrust and slowed new-hire proficiency. To address this, I led a redesign that reimagined training as an empowering, educational experience. Through stakeholder co-creation and systems mapping, we uncovered leverage points and modernized the experience.

Process

  1. Visualize the current traninig system Mapped the existing training system to uncover root causes.

  2. Humanaize the System Engaged stakeholders through interviews and co-design sessions to identify leverage points and align goals.

  3. Redesign the system Prototyped and tested redesigned solutions

Visualize the System

We visualize the system by creating network, process, and problem maps. Allowing us to understand complex connections and stakeholder relationships, analyze processes from various perspectives, and address root causes rather than symptoms..

Humanize the System

User interviews revealed deep mistrust in the training system. New hires viewed it as outdated, unreliable, and inconsistent. Most relied on personal networks over official resources. By mapping these behaviors, we reframed the challenge from fixing content to rebuilding trust through clarity, modern design, and guided learning experiences.

The Redesign

Prioritization matrix

We initiated the meeting with stakeholders by reviewing the maps and summarizing our collective learnings. Through co-creation and co-design discussions, we generated ideas to leverage redesigning the system. We identified that we could achieve this by updating the structure, processes, infrastructure, talent, or through incentives.

Focusing our efforts

The user is already familiar with in-app onboarding and training techniques from other applications. I can leverage some of those techniques to enhance the training experience.

Given the user's expertise in in-app onboarding and training, we propose updating our app with techniques from related industries. This initiative represents a medium effort with a potentially high impact. Current discussions within the team involve choosing between integrating an external app for onboarding or developing in-house tooltips and similar features manually.

Identify a Potential Prototype

We pinpointed a potential prototype: incorporating in-app guides and onboarding using metrics as our basis. Given users' familiarity with in-app onboarding from other apps, I can utilize similar techniques to enrich the experience.

Prototype Testing

Objectives

When prototyping, the aim isn't to determine whether an idea worked; it's to grasp the system comprehensively and strategize for the next experiment.

Reflection

The findings indicate that implementing in-app onboarding and guides improves outcomes and enhances user confidence in their job. Additionally, discrepancies in vocabulary across teams highlight the importance of standardizing terminology for clearer communication.

Conclusion

Results

By leading a cross-functional initiative and leveraging co-creation techniques with stakeholder insights, we redesigned Aspirion's training system.
